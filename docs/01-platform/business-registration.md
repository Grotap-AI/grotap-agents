# Business Registration

Business Registration

## Form an Anonymous Wyoming LLC
Wyoming is a top choice because it does not require the names of members or managers to be listed in the Articles of Organization filed with the Secretary of State.

Jones Health Law,
- Use a Professional Registered Agent: Hire a service to act as your registered agent . Their name and address will appear on public records instead of yours.
- Virtual Business Address: Use a virtual office or mail forwarding service  in Wyoming to provide a principal office address, keeping your home address off the record.
   Nominee Services: Some services offer "nominee managers" or "public record directors" who sign filings on your behalf, providing a further layer of separation.

   Wolters

2. Register the Domain through the LLC
Once the LLC is formed, use its information for all domain-related records.
- LLC as Registrant: Register the domain name using the LLC's name and its Wyoming business address as the owner (registrant).
- Domain Privacy (WHOIS Privacy): Always enable "WHOIS Privacy" or "Private Registration" at your registrar. This masks even the LLC's details from public search tools, replacing them with the registrar's proxy information.
   Email Masking: Use a generic administrative email (e.g., admin@yourllc.com) rather than one linked to your personal identity.

3. Maintain Anonymity in Operations
- Business Banking: Open a business bank account in the LLC's name. You will have to disclose your identity to the bank (due to "Know Your Customer" laws), but this information is not public.
- Website Policies: Ensure your website's Privacy Policy and Terms of Service list the LLC name, not your personal name.
   Avoid "DBA" if Privacy is the Goal: In many states, registering a "Doing Business As" (DBA) name requires a filing that links the trade name directly to the owner's name. It is often better to have the LLC own the brand directly.

   Small Business Administration (.gov) +4

## Important Legal Caveats
- Corporate Transparency Act (CTA): As of 2024, most U.S. small businesses must file a Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report  with FinCEN. This identifies the real owners to the federal government, though it remains confidential and is not accessible to the general public.
   Subpoenas: Anonymity protects you from "prying eyes" and automated searches, but it does not protect you from law enforcement or a valid court-ordered subpoena.
